# School Leader's NEP Readiness Survey — With FP Weights

**Project:** क्षितिज (Kshitij) — NEP-2020 Readiness Index
**Instrument Type:** School Leader Orientation Baseline
**Who:** School Heads / Academic Leaders
**Output:** FP Orientation (FP1–FP5) — No depth band (orientation only)

---

## FP Orientation Key

| Code | Principle | Core Question Leader Is Asking |
|------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| FP-1 | Every Child is Unique | "How is each learner experiencing learning?" |
| FP-2 | Holistic & Experiential Learning | "Is learning meaningful beyond textbooks?" |
| FP-3 | Teacher as Reflective Practitioner | "What is the teacher thinking, trying, learning?" |
| FP-4 | Assessment for Learning | "What does evidence tell us about learning gaps?" |
| FP-5 | Collaboration & Community | "Who else is part of the learning ecosystem?" |

**Design Note:** These are not levels. They are lenses of attention. Each option in a leader question maps to ONE dominant Foundational Philosophy orientation. It tells us where the leader's attention naturally goes first.

---

## Rubric Levels

| Level | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Level 1** | NEP-Aligned | Diagnostic, learner-centered, system-aware responses |
| **Level 2** | Partially Aligned | Responsive, partial awareness — on the path |
| **Level 3** | Traditional | Compliance-focused, procedural, or surface-level responses |

## Section 1: Each Child is Unique (FP-1)

### Q1. When reviewing classroom observations, which pattern concerns you the most?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Students finishing work at different times | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | Students asking many questions during the lesson | FP-4 | Level 2 |
| C | Students working quietly but showing little initiative | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Students discussing answers with peers | FP-5 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Recognises lack of student agency or engagement as the core issue
**Level 2:** Notices engagement inconsistently; partially aware
**Level 3:** Focus is on compliance, quietness, uniformity

### Q2. How do you usually understand students' varied learning preferences?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Through teacher conversations | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| B | Through exam performance trends | FP-4 | Level 3 |
| C | By noticing participation patterns in class visits | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | From parent feedback during meetings | FP-5 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Uses observation patterns to understand differences holistically
**Level 2:** Relies on teacher talk or parent feedback alone
**Level 3:** Uses exam scores as the primary indicator of learning needs

### Q3. When walking into a classroom, what do you naturally look at first?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Whether students are attentive | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | Whether the teacher is following the plan | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| C | Whether different students are engaged in different ways | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Whether the class is quiet and orderly | — | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Looks at differentiated engagement across students
**Level 2:** Looks at teacher actions more than student learning
**Level 3:** Prioritises silence and order over learning behaviour

## Section 2: Diagnosing Learning Levels (FP-4)

### Q4. If multiple classes struggle with a topic, what is your typical next step?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Check whether the chapters were completed | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | Compare the performance data across classes | FP-4 | Level 2 |
| C | Discuss with teachers how students approached the topic | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Ask teachers to schedule additional revision periods | FP-3 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Investigates student learning process and thinking
**Level 2:** Checks surface-level comparisons or revision
**Level 3:** Focuses on speed/coverage rather than understanding

### Q5. When looking at learning data, which trend do you investigate first?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Number of students who scored below 40% | FP-4 | Level 3 |
| B | Variation between question types | FP-4 | Level 2 |
| C | Comparisons between sections of the same grade | FP-4 | Level 2 |
| D | Shifts in performance of specific students over time | FP-1 | **Level 1** |

**Level 1:** Tracks progress of individual students over time
**Level 2:** Compares groups or question types
**Level 3:** Focuses only on low scores, not causes

### Q6. During a class observation, if students complete a task very quickly, you generally think:

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | The task was easy and appropriate | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | The teacher's explanation was very clear | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| C | The task may not have challenged all learners | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Students must have memorised similar questions | FP-4 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Questions task difficulty; checks if challenge was appropriate for all
**Level 2:** Assumes memorisation or over-simplicity
**Level 3:** Thinks speed = success; no depth-checking

## Section 3: Competency-Based Learning (FP-2)

### Q7. When reviewing lesson plans, which aspect do you notice most?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Whether the content matches the textbook | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | Whether the activities are manageable within the period | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| C | Whether the intended outcomes are measurable | FP-4 | **Level 1** |
| D | Whether instructions are clear for the teacher to follow | FP-3 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Focuses on whether outcomes are measurable (competency lens)
**Level 2:** Focuses on practicality or clarity of instructions
**Level 3:** Focuses mainly on textbook alignment

### Q8. A teacher asks for support in improving student understanding. Your first question is usually:

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | "Which part of the syllabus is pending?" | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | "What tasks did students struggle with?" | FP-4 | **Level 1** |
| C | "How did students respond during the lesson?" | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | "Which worksheets have been completed so far?" | — | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Seeks evidence of student thinking and misconceptions
**Level 2:** Seeks information on tasks or activities used
**Level 3:** Focuses on syllabus completion first

### Q9. When teachers prepare activities, what is your main consideration?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Whether the activity is manageable for all classes | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| B | Whether the activity matches the exam format | FP-4 | Level 3 |
| C | Whether students must think in different ways during the activity | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Whether the activity occupies the entire period | — | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Ensures cognitive engagement and varied thinking
**Level 2:** Ensures feasibility and basic engagement
**Level 3:** Prioritises order, time-fill, or exam mimicry

## Section 4: Teacher Upskilling (FP-3)

### Q10. When you see a teacher using an unfamiliar method, what do you usually do?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Check whether the class control is maintained | — | Level 3 |
| B | Observe student reactions closely | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| C | Ask the teacher to explain the thinking behind it later | FP-3 | **Level 1** |
| D | Remind them to stay aligned with the yearly plan | FP-2 | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Engages with the method through observation or inquiry
**Level 2:** Partial engagement — monitors but doesn't explore
**Level 3:** Focuses on control or compliance rather than innovation

### Q11. When a teacher struggles repeatedly with a concept, your instinctive response is:

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Ask another teacher to share materials | FP-5 | Level 2 |
| B | Suggest attending an upcoming workshop | FP-3 | Level 2 |
| C | Sit together to understand the root difficulty | FP-3 | **Level 1** |
| D | Recommend revising the chapter again | — | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Diagnoses the root difficulty collaboratively
**Level 2:** Provides support but not diagnostic support
**Level 3:** Suggests repetition without investigation

### Q12. During staff meetings, what do you tend to highlight the most?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Completion of planned activities | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| B | Instances of effective teamwork | FP-5 | Level 2 |
| C | Student progress trends | FP-4 | **Level 1** |
| D | Challenges teachers are facing | FP-3 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Highlights student learning trends as the primary agenda
**Level 2:** Highlights collaboration or challenges — important but not learning-centered
**Level 3:** Focuses on completion and compliance

## Section 5: Parent–Teacher Collaboration (FP-5)

### Q13. When planning parent interactions, what is your priority?

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Ensuring logistical arrangements are smooth | — | Level 3 |
| B | Updating parents about academic schedules | FP-2 | Level 2 |
| C | Helping parents understand how learning happens | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Addressing specific concerns raised by parents | FP-5 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Uses parent interactions to build understanding of the learning process
**Level 2:** Provides updates or responds to concerns — reactive, not proactive
**Level 3:** Focuses on logistics rather than learning partnerships

### Q14. When parents ask about their child's progress, you usually talk about:

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Attendance and behaviour | — | Level 3 |
| B | Upcoming school activities | FP-2 | Level 3 |
| C | How the child learns and participates | FP-1 | **Level 1** |
| D | Marks and test performance | FP-4 | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Discusses the child's learning process and participation
**Level 2:** (No option maps to Level 2 for this question)
**Level 3:** Discusses surface-level indicators (attendance, marks, activities)

### Q15. When designing the school communication plan, you focus on:

| Option | Text | FP | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------------|
| A | Frequency of communication | — | Level 3 |
| B | Ensuring messages are clear and short | FP-2 | Level 2 |
| C | Helping parents support learning at home | FP-5 | **Level 1** |
| D | Making sure all notices reach parents | — | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Designs communication to enable parents as learning partners
**Level 2:** Ensures clarity but not learning-partnership focus
**Level 3:** Focuses on delivery logistics rather than learning content
