## Practice Depth Bands

| Code | Band | Classroom Meaning |
|------|------|-------------------|
| D1 | Procedural | Rule-based, default, surface action |
| D2 | Responsive | Adjusts based on situation |

| D4 | Adaptive | Intentionally redesigns practice |

## Section 1: Each Child is Unique (FP-1)

### Q1. When two students learn at very different speeds, what do you usually do?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | Continue teaching at the same pace for everyone | FP-2 | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | Give extra homework to the slower learner | FP-1 | D1 | Level 3 |
| C | Provide varied support so both can progress | FP-1 | D3 | Level 1 |
| D | Ask the faster learner to help the slower one | FP-5 | D2 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Intentionally adapts instruction to individual needs (diagnostic differentiation)
**Level 2:** Uses peer support as a situational adjustment
**Level 3:** Applies uniform or surface-level responses to learner differences

### Q2. How do you understand a child's strengths or interests?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | By looking at test scores | FP-4 | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | By observing classroom behaviour and interactions | FP-1 | D3 | Level 1 |
| C | By asking parents during PTMs | FP-5 | D2 | Level 2 |
| D | I don't specifically track strengths or interests | — | D1 | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Uses observation as a diagnostic tool to understand learner diversity
**Level 2:** Relies on external feedback (parents) rather than direct observation
**Level 3:** Uses marks or does not track strengths at all

### Q3. How do you ensure shy students participate?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | Call them out randomly so they answer | FP-4 | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | Pair them with confident students | FP-5 | D2 | Level 2 |
| C | Create low-pressure activities where everyone contributes | FP-1 | D3 | Level 1 |
| D | Let them stay quiet — they will talk when ready | — | D1 | Level 3 |

**Level 1:** Designs inclusive participation structures based on learner profiles
**Level 2:** Uses peer-based support as a responsive strategy
**Level 3:** Uses pressure or avoidance rather than inclusive design

## Section 2: Diagnosing Learning Levels (FP-4)

### Q4. Before starting a new chapter, how do you check learning levels?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | I don't — everyone starts fresh | — | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | By giving a quick informal check (questions/activity) | FP-4 | D2 | Level 1 |
| C | By checking the previous exam scores | FP-4 | D1 | Level 2 |
| D | By asking students if they already know the topic | FP-1 | D1 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Uses formative, informal checks to understand readiness before teaching
**Level 2:** Uses existing data or self-report — partial diagnostic intent
**Level 3:** No pre-assessment; assumes uniform starting point

### Q5. When a child performs poorly, how do you find the cause?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | Assume the child didn't study | — | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | Re-check the question paper | FP-4 | D1 | Level 2 |
| C | Use small diagnostic tasks to find exact gaps | FP-4 | D3 | Level 1 |
| D | Talk to the parent first | FP-5 | D2 | Level 2 |

**Level 1:** Uses targeted diagnostic tasks to identify specific learning gaps
**Level 2:** Checks instrument or seeks external input — partial investigation
**Level 3:** Attributes failure to student effort without investigation

### Q6. How often do you assess progress during teaching?

| Option | Text | FP | Depth | Rubric Level |
|--------|------|----|-------|-------------|
| A | Only during exams | FP-4 | D1 | Level 3 |
| B | Only when I feel students are slow | FP-4 | D1 | Level 3 |
| C | Weekly informal checks | FP-4 | D2 | Level 2 |
| D | Daily quick checks (exit slips, oral checks, tasks) | FP-4 | D3 | Level 1 |

**Level 1:** Embeds continuous, daily formative assessment into teaching practice
**Level 2:** Periodic informal checks — responsive but not systematic
**Level 3:** Assessment only at formal or reactive moments

| **Level 2** | Partially Aligned | Responsive, situational adjustments — on the path (typically D2) |
| **Level 3** | Traditional / Pre-NEP | Procedural, compliance-based, or avoidant responses (typically D1) |
