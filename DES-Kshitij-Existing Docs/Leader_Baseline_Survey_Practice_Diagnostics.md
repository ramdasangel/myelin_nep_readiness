# School Leader Baseline Survey — NEP-2020 Culture & System Readiness Practice Diagnostics

**Project:** क्षितिज (Kshitij) — NEP-2020 Readiness Index
**Instrument Type:** Practice Diagnostics — Leadership Lens
**Who:** School Heads / Academic Leaders
**Framing Shown to Leaders:** *"This section focuses on leadership structures, decision-making, and culture that enable NEP-aligned learning."*
**Response Scale:** Strongly Agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly Disagree
**SRI Construct Fed:** System Readiness (Weight: 20/100)

---

## How This Instrument Feeds the SRI

This survey captures **Practice Diagnostic Signals from the leadership perspective** — the structural and cultural conditions that determine whether NEP-aligned practice can sustain across the school.

The interpretive stack reads:

```
Orientation (Leader + Teacher) → Practice Depth (Teacher) → Practice Diagnostics (this instrument)
```

The Leadership Lens (B1–B5) complements the Teacher Lens (A1–A4). Together they compose the **System Readiness** construct. Where they diverge, the gap itself is a signal:

- **Leader says "Strong" + Teacher says "Weak"** → Leaders believe systems exist but teachers don't experience them
- **Leader says "Weak" + Teacher says "Strong"** → Informal support exists despite no formal structure
- **Both say "Weak"** → Systemic gap — neither structure nor culture supports this area
- **Both say "Strong"** → Validated enablement — this area is a readiness strength

### Scoring & Banding Rules

**Per-Question Scoring:**

| Response | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Strongly Agree | 4 |
| Agree | 3 |
| Disagree | 2 |
| Strongly Disagree | 1 |

**Per-Area Aggregation:**
- Area Score = Mean of all sub-area scores within the area

**Area-Level Banding:**

| Band | Mean Score Range | Label | Interpretation |
|------|-----------------|-------|----------------|
| Band 1 | 1.0 – 1.99 | Weak enablement | Leadership structures are absent or ineffective for this area |
| Band 2 | 2.0 – 2.99 | Partial enablement | Some structures exist but are inconsistent or under-resourced |
| Band 3 | 3.0 – 3.49 | Adequate enablement | Functioning structures in place; refinement opportunities exist |
| Band 4 | 3.5 – 4.0 | Strong enablement | Leadership actively drives and sustains this area |

## Area B1: NEP Governance & Ownership

**Purpose:** Captures whether NEP implementation has clear ownership, translation into actionable plans, and distributed responsibility.
**SET Reading Rule:** B1 explains whether NEP is a stated priority or an operationalised commitment.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** Cross-FP — this is the governance backbone for all FPs.

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Leadership View) |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| B1.1 | Ownership | You have a clearly identified role or team responsible for NEP implementation. |
| B1.2 | Translation | NEP priorities are translated into term-wise focus areas. |
| B1.3 | Distribution | Responsibility for NEP implementation is shared across the school. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + B1 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| High leader orientation across FPs | B1 Ownership ↓ | NEP is rhetorically endorsed but nobody owns execution — **governance vacuum**. |
| High leader orientation | B1 Translation ↓ | NEP priorities are spoken about generally but not broken into term-wise actionables — **strategy-action gap**. |
| Teacher depth at D1–D2 | B1 Distribution ↓ | NEP implementation sits with leadership alone; teachers experience it as top-down — **ownership bottleneck**. |
| Any configuration | B1 all sub-areas ↑ | NEP has a named owner, actionable plans, and distributed responsibility — **governance is active**. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Ownership | No identified person/team responsible for NEP — it floats | Clear ownership structure for NEP implementation |
| Translation | NEP remains abstract; not broken into term-wise actions | Priorities are operationalised into focus areas per term |
| Distribution | Implementation is centralised; teachers don't share ownership | Responsibility is distributed across the school |

## Area B2: Data-Informed Decision Culture

**Purpose:** Captures whether learning data flows upward and influences leadership decisions.
**SET Reading Rule:** B2 explains whether assessment data is used structurally or remains in teacher notebooks.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-4 (Assessment for Learning)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Leadership View) |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| B2.1 | Review | You review learning diagnostic insights at leadership level. |
| B2.2 | Decisions | Academic decisions are influenced by learning trends. |
| B2.3 | Support | Teachers are supported when data suggests changes are needed. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + B2 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| FP-4 depth at D2–D3 (teachers) | B2 Review ↓ | Teachers diagnose learning gaps but leadership doesn't engage with the data — **data ceiling**. |
| FP-4 depth at D1 | B2 Decisions ↓ | Neither teachers nor leaders use learning data for decisions — **assessment is ritual**. |
| Teacher A1 high (tools exist) | B2 Support ↓ | Teachers have diagnostic tools but leadership doesn't follow through when data demands change — **action gap at leadership level**. |
| All signals aligned | B2 all ↑ | Data flows from classroom → leadership → decisions → teacher support — **data-informed loop is closed**. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Review | Learning data doesn't reach leadership conversations | Leadership regularly reviews diagnostic insights |
| Decisions | Academic decisions are disconnected from learning evidence | Learning trends actively shape decisions |
| Support | Teachers are left alone even when data shows they need help | Teachers receive support when data signals a need for change |

## Area B3: Teacher Development Culture (Leadership View)

**Purpose:** Captures whether leadership creates conditions for teacher growth — peer learning, safety, and experimentation.
**SET Reading Rule:** B3 is the leadership mirror of Teacher Area A2. Divergence signals perception gaps.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-3 (Reflective Practitioner) + FP-5 (Collaboration)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Leadership View) |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| B3.1 | Peer Learning Circles | You have regular and purposeful teacher reflection forums. |
| B3.2 | Safety | Teachers are encouraged to share challenges without fear. |
| B3.3 | Experimentation | Experimentation is protected and valued by leadership. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + B3 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| FP-3 at D1 (teachers) | B3 Safety ↓ (leader says low too) | Both sides agree — reflection is unsafe. **Culture blocker confirmed.** |
| FP-3 at D1 (teachers) | B3 Safety ↑ (leader says high) | Leaders believe safety exists but teachers don't experience it — **perception gap**. This is more dangerous than mutual low because it hides the problem. |
| FP-5 at D1 | B3 Peer Learning ↓ | Collaboration structures don't exist — teachers work in isolation by design, not choice. |
| FP-3 at D3+ | B3 Experimentation ↑ | Reflective teachers are protected by leadership — **growth is structurally enabled**. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Peer Learning Circles | No regular, purposeful reflection forums for teachers | PLCs or reflection forums are regular and meaningful |
| Safety | Teachers fear judgment when sharing challenges | Teachers can openly discuss difficulties |
| Experimentation | Trying new approaches is risky — failures are punished | Innovation is protected and valued by leadership |

### Cross-Read: B3 vs. Teacher A2

| B3 (Leader View) | A2 (Teacher View) | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| High | High | Validated — growth culture is real and experienced |
| High | Low | **Perception gap** — leaders think culture is safe; teachers disagree |
| Low | High | Informal support exists despite lack of formal structures |
| Low | Low | **Confirmed gap** — no growth culture from either perspective |

## Area B4: HPC & Reporting Culture

**Purpose:** Captures whether HPC is treated as a learning narrative or just a reporting requirement.
**SET Reading Rule:** B4 is the leadership mirror of Teacher Area A3. Together they reveal whether HPC is alive or bureaucratic.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-4 (Assessment for Learning) + FP-1 (Every Child is Unique)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Leadership View) |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| B4.1 | Purpose | HPC is discussed as a learning narrative, not just reporting. |
| B4.2 | Parent Clarity | Parents are oriented to what HPC represents. |
| B4.3 | Teacher Support | Teachers are supported in shifting beyond marks-based reporting. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + B4 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| FP-4 depth low | B4 Purpose ↓ | HPC is treated as a reporting artefact — neither leadership nor teachers use it diagnostically. |
| FP-1 at D2 | B4 Parent Clarity ↓ | Teachers are beginning to notice learner diversity, but parents don't understand HPC — **communication gap blocks ecosystem readiness**. |
| Any depth | B4 Teacher Support ↑ | Leadership is actively helping teachers move beyond marks — **transition is being managed**. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Purpose | HPC = compliance artefact; discussed as reporting only | HPC is framed as a learning narrative |
| Parent Clarity | Parents don't understand what HPC captures or means | Parents are oriented to the HPC framework |
| Teacher Support | Teachers are expected to shift but given no support | Active support for the transition from marks to holistic reporting |

### Cross-Read: B4 vs. Teacher A3

| B4 (Leader View) | A3 (Teacher View) | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| High | High | HPC is genuinely formative — both sides experience it as meaningful |
| High | Low | Leaders see HPC as purposeful but teachers find it confusing or time-consuming |
| Low | High | Teachers understand HPC but leadership hasn't created the culture around it |
| Low | Low | HPC is paper compliance on both sides |

## Area B5: Parent & Community Partnerships (Leadership View)

**Purpose:** Captures whether parent/community engagement is strategically planned or left to chance.
**SET Reading Rule:** B5 is the leadership mirror of Teacher Area A4. Together they reveal whether collaboration is systemically supported.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-5 (Collaboration & Community)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Leadership View) |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| B5.1 | Strategy | You have a school-level approach to parent engagement. |
| B5.2 | Activation | Community resources are identified and used intentionally. |
| B5.3 | Feedback | Parent feedback is used to improve learning processes. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + B5 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Low FP-5 depth | B5 Strategy ↓ | No school-level approach to parent engagement — collaboration is incidental. |
| Low FP-5 depth | B5 Activation ↓ | Community resources are not identified — the school operates in isolation. |
| FP-5 at D2+ | B5 Feedback ↑ | Parent input is actively used — **collaboration loop is closing**. |
| Teacher A4 low | B5 Strategy ↑ | Leadership has a strategy but teachers don't experience support — **execution gap**. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Strategy | No deliberate approach to parent engagement | School has an intentional parent engagement plan |
| Activation | Community resources are unidentified or unused | Community resources are mapped and activated |
| Feedback | Parent feedback is not collected or not used | Parent feedback actively improves learning processes |

### Cross-Read: B5 vs. Teacher A4

| B5 (Leader View) | A4 (Teacher View) | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| High | High | Parent engagement is both planned and experienced — systemic strength |
| High | Low | Strategy exists on paper but teachers feel unsupported in execution |
| Low | High | Teachers make it work informally despite no institutional strategy |
| Low | Low | Parent engagement is structurally absent |

## Open Reflection Question

**Purpose:** Captures leadership voice on the single highest-leverage system change.
**Use in SRI:** Qualitative signal; not scored but used for narrative insight and playbook co-creation.

## Full Aggregation: System Readiness Construct in SRI

### Teacher Lens (A1–A4) + Leadership Lens (B1–B5) → System Readiness

| Component | Areas | Weight in SRI |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Teacher Practice Diagnostics | A1, A2, A3, A4 | Part of System Readiness (20/100) |
| Leader Practice Diagnostics | B1, B2, B3, B4, B5 | Part of System Readiness (20/100) |

### The Five Gap Types (Emergent from Cross-Reading)

| Gap Type | How to Detect | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Tool Gap** | A1 low + High FP-4 orientation | Intent exists, tools don't |
| **Culture Gap** | A2 Safety/Experimentation low + FP-3 stalls at D1 | Environment blocks growth |
| **Comprehension Gap** | A3 Understanding/Clarity low + Low FP-4 depth | HPC/assessment is form-filling, not formative |
| **Structural Gap** | A4 Facilitation low + Low FP-5 depth | Collaboration desired but systemically unsupported |
| **Translation Gap** | All areas high + Depth still D1–D2 | System is enabled but practice hasn't shifted — look at cognitive capacity |

### Key Principle

> System Readiness is NOT about whether the school "has good systems."
> It is about whether the conditions surrounding classroom practice make depth progression **possible, safe, and sustained**.
