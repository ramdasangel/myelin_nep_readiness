# Teacher Baseline Survey — NEP-2020 Enablement & Systems Practice Diagnostics

**Project:** क्षितिज (Kshitij) — NEP-2020 Readiness Index
**Instrument Type:** Practice Diagnostics — Teacher Lens
**Who:** All Teachers
**Framing Shown to Teachers:** *"This section focuses on the systems and supports available to you — not your classroom practices."*
**Response Scale:** Strongly Agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly Disagree
**SRI Construct Fed:** System Readiness (Weight: 20/100)

---

## How This Instrument Feeds the SRI

This survey captures **Practice Diagnostic Signals** — the conditions surrounding practice as experienced by teachers. It sits in the interpretive stack as:

```
Orientation → Practice Depth → Practice Diagnostics (this instrument)
```

Practice Diagnostics explain **why** observed practice depth looks the way it does. They are the system-reality layer — they do not define orientation and should never be read in isolation.

### Scoring & Banding Rules

**Per-Question Scoring:**

| Response | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Strongly Agree | 4 |
| Agree | 3 |
| Disagree | 2 |
| Strongly Disagree | 1 |

**Per-Area Aggregation:**
- Area Score = Mean of all sub-area scores within the area (e.g., mean of Access, Usability, Follow-through, Collective Use, Student Clarity for A1)

**Area-Level Banding:**

| Band | Mean Score Range | Label | Interpretation |
|------|-----------------|-------|----------------|
| Band 1 | 1.0 – 1.99 | Weak enablement | System is not supporting this area |
| Band 2 | 2.0 – 2.99 | Partial enablement | Some support exists but gaps are significant |
| Band 3 | 3.0 – 3.49 | Adequate enablement | Support exists; refinement may be needed |
| Band 4 | 3.5 – 4.0 | Strong enablement | System actively supports and sustains this area |

**Design Note:** No FP is directly tagged on these questions. Areas are not FP-based — they are condition-based. However, each area has a canonical link to FP depth (see SET Reading Rules below).

## Area A1: Continuous Learning Diagnostics (Teacher Enablement)

**Purpose:** Captures whether teachers can see learning clearly enough to act diagnostically.
**SET Reading Rule:** A1 explains whether teachers have the visibility and tools needed for diagnostic practice.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-4 (Assessment for Learning)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Teacher View) |
|---|----------|------------------------|
| A1.1 | Access | You have access to simple tools or formats to understand students' learning levels. |
| A1.2 | Usability | These tools help you clearly identify what students are struggling with. |
| A1.3 | Follow-through | You get time or support to act on diagnostic insights. |
| A1.4 | Collective Use | Diagnostic insights are discussed with other teachers (meetings / Peer Learning Circles). |
| A1.5 | Student Clarity | Students are informed about what they are working towards based on diagnostics. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + A1 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Low FP-4 depth | A1 Access ↓, Usability ↓ | Teachers value assessment but lack usable tools to identify learning gaps. |
| Low FP-4 depth | A1 Follow-through ↓ | Diagnostic information exists, but time/support to act is missing. |
| FP-4 stalls at D2 | A1 Collective Use ↓ | Assessment remains individual and does not mature through shared sense-making. |
| Low FP-1 depth | A1 Student Clarity ↓ | Teachers attend to diversity but students don't know their own learning goals — the loop is open. |
| FP-4 at D3+ | A1 all sub-areas ↑ | Diagnostic culture is enabled and embedded — a readiness strength. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Access | Teachers cannot initiate diagnosis — tools are missing or inaccessible | Tools exist; check if they're actually used (Usability) |
| Usability | Tools exist but don't reveal actionable learning gaps | Tools provide clear, usable diagnostic insights |
| Follow-through | Diagnosis happens but nothing changes — action gap | Teachers have time and support to act on findings |
| Collective Use | Assessment stays private; no peer sense-making | Diagnostic data is discussed in PLCs or meetings |
| Student Clarity | Students don't know their own learning targets | Students are oriented toward their own goals |

## Area A2: Teacher Development & Growth Support

**Purpose:** Captures whether a growth culture exists for teachers beyond compliance training.
**SET Reading Rule:** A2 explains whether teacher reflection and experimentation can sustain.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-3 (Reflective Practitioner)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Teacher View) |
|---|----------|------------------------|
| A2.1 | Structure | You have a clear plan or pathway for your professional growth aligned to NEP. |
| A2.2 | Safety | You have safe spaces to discuss classroom challenges openly. |
| A2.3 | Peer Learning | You have opportunities to learn from other teachers regularly. |
| A2.4 | Experimentation | You are encouraged to try new approaches without fear of evaluation. |
| A2.5 | Growth Culture | Teacher growth is discussed beyond annual reviews. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + A2 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| FP-3 at D1 | A2 Safety ↓, Experimentation ↓ | Reflection is stated but unsafe to act on — the environment inhibits depth progression. |
| FP-3 at D2 | A2 Peer Learning ↓ | Teachers reflect individually but lack peer structures to deepen insight. |
| FP-3 at D3+ | A2 all sub-areas ↑ | Reflective practice is supported and social — a readiness accelerator. |
| High FP-3 orientation | A2 Structure ↓ | Teachers want to grow but see no clear pathway — intent without scaffolding. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Structure | No clear professional growth pathway — ad hoc development | NEP-aligned growth pathway exists |
| Safety | Challenging to share failures or struggles — fear of judgment | Open culture where challenges can be discussed |
| Peer Learning | Isolated practice — no regular teacher-to-teacher learning | Regular peer learning opportunities exist |
| Experimentation | Innovation is risky — fear of evaluation discourages trying new things | Experimentation is encouraged and protected |
| Growth Culture | Growth only discussed at annual review — episodic | Ongoing growth conversations — embedded culture |

## Area A3: Holistic Progress Card (HPC) — Teacher Enablement

**Purpose:** Captures whether teachers understand and can use HPC beyond marks-based reporting.
**SET Reading Rule:** A3 explains whether HPC is a living diagnostic tool or a compliance artefact.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-4 (Assessment for Learning) + FP-1 (Every Child is Unique)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Teacher View) |
|---|----------|------------------------|
| A3.1 | Understanding | You clearly understand what to observe beyond marks for HPC. |
| A3.2 | Clarity | HPC indicators are clearly explained and documented. |
| A3.3 | Time | You get time to record and reflect on HPC inputs. |
| A3.4 | Use | HPC discussions are used to guide learning conversations. |
| A3.5 | Student Role | Students are involved in reflecting on their own progress. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + A3 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Low FP-4 depth | A3 Understanding ↓ | Teachers don't know what to observe beyond marks — HPC is a form, not a practice. |
| FP-1 at D2 | A3 Student Role ↓ | Teachers notice diversity but students are passive in their own progress story. |
| FP-4 at D3+ | A3 Use ↑ | HPC informs instructional conversations — assessment is diagnostic and formative. |
| Any FP depth | A3 Time ↓ | Even willing teachers can't sustain HPC practice without protected time. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Understanding | Teachers don't know what to observe beyond marks | Clear understanding of HPC observation criteria |
| Clarity | HPC indicators are vague or undocumented | Indicators are well-explained and accessible |
| Time | No protected time for HPC recording/reflection | Adequate time allocated for HPC processes |
| Use | HPC is filed, not used for learning conversations | HPC actively guides teaching and learning decisions |
| Student Role | Students are objects of assessment, not participants | Students reflect on and contribute to their own progress |

## Area A4: Parent & Community Support (Teacher Experience)

**Purpose:** Captures whether parent engagement is enabled by the system or dumped on individual teachers.
**SET Reading Rule:** A4 explains whether FP-5 (Collaboration) can develop or is structurally blocked.
**Primary FP Depth Link:** FP-5 (Collaboration & Community)

| # | Sub-Area | Question (Teacher View) |
|---|----------|------------------------|
| A4.1 | Communication | You feel supported in communicating learning progress to parents. |
| A4.2 | Guidance | You receive clear guidance on how to engage parents meaningfully. |
| A4.3 | Facilitation | Community interactions are facilitated by the school (not left to you alone). |
| A4.4 | Recognition | Efforts to involve parents or community are recognised. |

### SET Canonical Interpretation Sentences (Examples)

| Depth Observation | + A4 Signal | → Canonical Reading |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Low FP-5 depth | A4 Facilitation ↓ | Collaboration is valued but structurally unsupported — teachers carry the load alone. |
| FP-5 at D1 | A4 Guidance ↓ | Teachers want to engage parents but don't know how — intent without method. |
| FP-5 at D2+ | A4 Recognition ↑ | When collaboration efforts are seen and valued, they sustain and deepen. |

### Sub-Area Diagnostic Logic

| Sub-Area | If Low (≤2) | If High (≥3) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Communication | Teachers struggle to communicate progress beyond marks | Supported in sharing meaningful learning updates |
| Guidance | No clear methods for meaningful parent engagement | Clear guidance on engaging parents in learning |
| Facilitation | Community engagement falls entirely on individual teachers | School facilitates and structures community interactions |
| Recognition | Parent engagement efforts go unnoticed | Efforts are recognised and valued by the institution |

## Open Reflection Question

> *What support, if strengthened, would most help you improve learning for your students this term?*

**Purpose:** Captures unstructured teacher voice — may reveal enablement gaps not covered by A1–A4.
**Use in SRI:** Qualitative signal; not scored but used for narrative insight in SET interpretation.

## Aggregation to SRI: System Readiness Construct

| Area | Weight within System Readiness | What It Measures |
|------|-------------------------------|------------------|
| A1: Continuous Learning Diagnostics | Equal | Visibility into learning — can teachers see gaps? |
| A2: Teacher Development & Growth | Equal | Growth conditions — can teachers improve safely? |
| A3: HPC Teacher Enablement | Equal | Reporting quality — is assessment formative or ritual? |
| A4: Parent & Community Support | Equal | Collaboration infrastructure — is it enabled or dumped? |

**System Readiness** contributes **20 points** to the total SRI (100-point scale).
Teacher Lens (A1–A4) and Leadership Lens (B1–B5) together compose the System Readiness construct.

### Cross-Reading with Other Instruments

| If This Survey Shows... | And Orientation/Depth Shows... | Then Read As... |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| A1 low across all sub-areas | High FP-4 orientation | Intent for assessment exists but the system hasn't provided tools — **tool gap** |
| A2 Safety + Experimentation low | FP-3 stalls at D1 | Teachers want to reflect but the environment makes it unsafe — **culture gap** |
| A3 Understanding + Clarity low | Low FP-4 depth | HPC is a form-filling exercise, not a diagnostic practice — **comprehension gap** |
| A4 Facilitation low | Low FP-5 depth | Collaboration is desired but teachers carry it alone — **structural gap** |
| All areas high (Band 3–4) | Depth still at D1–D2 | System is enabled but practice hasn't shifted — **translation gap** (look at cognitive capacity) |
